GDPR

You should know basics of GDPR

Why it matters: Ignoring GDPR regulation can result to fines of up to million euros, or up to 4% of the organisation’s turnover for the previous financial year, whichever is greater. A lot of privacy requirements are based around GDPR.

The background: The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a privacy and security law in with the purpose of protecting an individual’s data and privacy within the EU and the EEA. The GDPR’s main aim is to maximise an individual’s control over their personal and sensible information, while also simplifying the regulatory environment for international business.

Is your privacy policy sufficient?

Why it matters: A clear privacy policy that is easy to find is a requirement under the GDPR.

In practice:

  • There are numerous privacy policy templates available online, but many of them fail to meet the GDPR requirements of clear and plain language.
  • The typical placement of a link to a privacy policy is in the footer at the bottom of the page. By following this convention, your users can find it easily.
